The MARBLE Project

Research Working Framework

Methodological Definition: This site indexes public newspaper phrase appearances of the specific text string "hearing voices" from 1888 to the present. It tracks public language trends and archive density, not clinical or medical diagnoses.

Central Research Hypotheses

  • The UCA Hypothesis: Evaluating organized linguistic patterns via Unidentified Communication Agents as a working research model.
  • The Inside Out Process: Tracking how private, internal experiences are translated into formal institutional records.
  • Wartime Suppression: Analyzing how macro-geopolitical events (WWI and WWII) systematically displaced civilian phrase reporting in the press.

Historical Timeline & Trends

  • 1888: Established operational archive baseline based on press geographical distribution.
  • 1904–1912: Measurable surge in historical public reporting trends.
  • 1914–1918 & 1938–1944: Sharp declines correlating directly with major wartime media mobilization.
  • 1989–2002: Peak archive phrase density, followed by a steady macro decline.

Full Report: Hearing Voices, Public Reporting Patterns, War-Time Suppression, and the MARBLE / UCA Hypothesis

This report organizes the Hearing Voices project into a historical, newspaper-archive, legal-medical, and threat-assessment framework.

The core dataset is not a clinical dataset of actual auditory hallucination events. It is a public-language dataset based on newspaper search results for the exact phrase “hearing voices.” The most precise description is: newspaper phrase appearances, not verified real-world voice-hearing incidents.

The working hypothesis preserved in this report is that MARBLE places the beginning of organized, human-sourced voice targeting around 1912. The alleged voices are identified by the project as UCAs — Unidentified Communication Agents — and are treated as a research hypothesis, not as a proven conclusion.

The strongest defensible conclusion is that the public record of “hearing voices” appears to be shaped by war, media attention, psychiatric language, legal reporting, archive structure, drug-era narratives, and institutional framing.

The next research step is article-level sampling. The graph gives the pattern. The articles will give the meaning.
